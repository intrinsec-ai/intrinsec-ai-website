Use Cases

Enterprise · AI Governance

Define who your agents are. Detect everything that drifts.

AI agents are replacing the tools employees use every day — and not all of them are sanctioned. You need one answer for every agent in your org: does what this agent receives match what it should? Give Intrinsec a plain-language description of what your agents are supposed to do. Everything that doesn't fit gets surfaced.

  • ◆ Org identity profile — describe your sanctioned agents in natural language: what they do, who they serve, what they should never touch
  • ◆ Intent divergence, not rules — you can't write a rule for every way an agent drifts. Intrinsec detects deviation from a modeled identity, so novel attacks and unsanctioned behavior surface automatically.
  • ◆ Org trust posture — aggregate drift across every agent to see where your entire AI footprint is pulling away from policy, not just individual incidents

See the Identity Plane in Action

A lightweight reference implementation as a plugin for OpenClaw — the popular self-hosted AI agent gateway. Observational by design.

@intrinsec-ai/openclaw-identity-plane

Identity Watchdog for OpenClaw Agents

Calibrates against your agent's cognitive files (SOUL.md, AGENTS.md, etc.) to build an identity anchor — a model of what your agent is supposed to do. Every inbound message is scored against that anchor. Drift is visible on a live dashboard — nothing is blocked, nothing is intercepted.

What it does
  • Identity calibration from cognitive files
  • Per-message drift scoring
  • Live drift dashboard with time-series chart
  • Identity compaction — LLM audit narratives grounded in named anchors
  • File-change annotations on the drift timeline
What it isn't
  • Lossless — subtle injection in long context can average out
  • Multi-tenant or fleet-scale
  • Persistent across long horizons
  • Multimodal
